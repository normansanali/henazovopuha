Kendall Jenner's 2018 Met Gala Dress

If you’re looking for A-list models to make a red carpet statement, look no further than Kendall Jenner.

The 22-year-old runway regular arrived at the N.Y.C. Metropolitan Museum of Art on Monday for the 2018 Met Gala and, of course, she definitely topped herself. Instead of turning to a barely-there naked dress as she's done in the past, Jenner surprised us and instead wore an off-the-shoulder white long-sleeve Off-White bustier with a matching set of wide-leg trousers that grazed the floor plus a set of gloves and Tiffany & Co. jewels. Interestingly, it looks like she may have taken cues from big sister Kim Kardashian, who last year wore a dreamy white off-the-shoulder Vivienne Westwood design.

Ahead of her arrival, Jenner got ready with Off-White designer Virgil Abloh.

Before she posed for the cameras, Jenner shared Instagram Stories footage of her pre-party meal, which apparently included French fries and what appeared to be a grilled cheese sandwich, peanut butter and jelly sandwiches, and a quesadilla. While getting her glam done, she posed in sweats and drank wine, just like any other girl would.

Last year, Jenner made jaws drop when she hit the carpet and a super sheer La Perla look that left very little to the imagination. It featured strategic cutouts plus an extraordinarily high-slit dress. Just one year before, she took a similar peek-a-boo approach in head-to-toe Versace that helped her flaunt every little detail.
