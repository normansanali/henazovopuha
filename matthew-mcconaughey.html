Career

Matthew McConaughey commenced the journey towards his filming career in 1992 as a director of the short film “Chicano Chariots”. He played the role of “Guy #2” in the 1993 American zombie film “My Boyfriend’s Back”, appeared as “David Wooderson” in the 1993 American coming-of-age comedy film “Dazed and Confused” and as “Ben Williams” in the 1994 comedy film “Angels in the Outfield”.

In 1995, he portrayed the character “Vilmer Slaughter” in the American slasher film “Texas Chainsaw Massacre: The Next Generation”, as  “Officer Abe Lincoln” in the comedy-drama film “Boys on the Side”, as “Rental Truck Guy” in the comedy-drama film “Glory Daze” and as “EI Rojo” in the crime thriller film “Scorpion Spring”.

He landed the character of “Buddy Deeds” in the 1996 mystery film “Lone Star”, as “Jake Brigance” in the 1996 legal drama film “A Time to Kill”, and as “Tip Tucker” in the 1996 road comedy film “Larger than Life”. He also appeared as “Palmer Joss” in the science fiction drama film “Contact” and as “Roger Sherman Baldwin” in the historical drama film “Amistad” in 1997.
