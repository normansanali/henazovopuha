Marc Blucas is an American actor and basketball player. He is known for his roles on the series Buffy the Vampire Slayer and Necessary Roughness, and in the films Wes Craven Presents: They, I Capture the Castle, Prey for Rock & Roll, First Daughter (2004), Three (2006), The Killing Floor, After Sex, The Jane Austen Book Club, Deadline (2009), Touchback, Brawl in Cell Block 99, Looking Glass (2018), and Unearth. He played basketball with the Wake Forest Demon Deacons college team.

His father is of half Polish and half Slovak descent. His mother is of half English and Scottish, and half Serbian, ancestry. Marc is married to journalist Ryan Haddon.

Marc’s paternal grandfather was Walter J. Blucas (the son of Peter/Pete Martin Blucas and Katherine/Kathryn/Catherine Caroline Kendelski/Kendelsky/Kendaleski). Walter was born in Pennsylvania, to Polish parents.

Marc’s paternal grandmother was Alice Phillips (the daughter of Charles Phillips and Barbara Suhar). Alice was born in Pennsylvania, to Slovak parents. Charles was the son of Georgii Fylyp and Maria Petrick.

Marc’s maternal grandfather was Elmer E. Gordon (the son of Harry Gordon and Mary Christine Long). Elmer was born in Ohio, and had Scottish and English ancestry. Harry was the son of Mary Dill. Marc’s great-grandmother Mary Christine was the daughter of Elmer E. Long and Cora Mae McKane.

Marc’s maternal grandmother is Dorothy Brdar (the daughter of Rade/Rode Brdar and Katherine/Kathryn/Catherine Zetz). Dorothy was born in Pennsylvania, to Serbian parents.
