The Top 10 Michigan Wolverines Basketball Players of All-Time

The University of Michigan's basketball program has a rich history, producing numerous talented players who have left a lasting impact on the sport. From legendary scorers to defensive stalwarts, here are the top 10 Michigan Wolverines basketball players of all time.

1. Cazzie Russell

Cazzie Russell is widely regarded as one of the greatest players in Michigan basketball history. He led the Wolverines to three consecutive Big Ten titles from 1964 to 1966 and was a two-time consensus All-American. Russell's scoring prowess and versatility made him a standout player during his collegiate career.

2. Chris Webber

Chris Webber made an immediate impact at Michigan, helping the Wolverines reach the NCAA Championship Game in 1992 and 1993. His athleticism, rebounding ability, and passing skills set him apart, earning him multiple All-American honors and a lasting legacy in Michigan basketball lore.

3. Glenn Rice

Glenn Rice's scoring exploits during his time at Michigan are legendary. He led the Wolverines to the NCAA Championship in 1989, claiming the tournament's Most Outstanding Player award. Rice's sharpshooting and offensive prowess cemented his status as one of the greatest Michigan players of all time.

4. Rudy Tomjanovich

Rudy Tomjanovich was a force to be reckoned with during his tenure at Michigan. A versatile forward with an impressive inside-outside game, Tomjanovich's impact on both ends of the floor was instrumental in the Wolverines' success. His stellar college career laid the foundation for a successful NBA career as both a player and coach.

5. Juwan Howard

Juwan Howard's impact on Michigan basketball extends beyond his playing days. As a member of the "Fab Five," Howard helped revolutionize college basketball while showcasing his exceptional skills on the court. His leadership, scoring ability, and defensive tenacity make him a standout figure in Michigan's basketball history.

6. Glen Rice

Glen Rice's scoring exploits during his time at Michigan are legendary. He led the Wolverines to the NCAA Championship in 1989, claiming the tournament's Most Outstanding Player award. Rice's sharpshooting and offensive prowess cemented his status as one of the greatest Michigan players of all time.

7. Jalen Rose

Jalen Rose's impact on Michigan basketball transcends statistics. A key member of the "Fab Five," Rose's playmaking ability, court vision, and leadership skills left an indelible mark on the program. His influence on the game and the culture surrounding it solidifies his place among the top Michigan players of all time.

8. Trey Burke

Trey Burke's remarkable 2012-2013 season earned him numerous accolades, including the Wooden Award and the Naismith College Player of the Year. Burke's electrifying playmaking, scoring ability, and clutch performances endeared him to Michigan fans and solidified his status as one of the program's all-time greats.

9. Rickey Green

Rickey Green's speed, defensive prowess, and playmaking ability set him apart during his time at Michigan. A two-time All-Big Ten selection, Green's impact on both ends of the floor and his contributions to the Wolverines' success make him a noteworthy figure in the program's history.

10. Bill Buntin

Bill Buntin's dominance in the paint and his rebounding prowess are etched in Michigan basketball folklore. A two-time All-American, Buntin's impact on the program during the early 1960s remains a testament to his enduring legacy as one of the top Michigan players of all time.
