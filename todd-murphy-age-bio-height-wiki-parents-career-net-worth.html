Todd Murphy Biography

Todd Murphy (born 15 November 2000) is an Australian cricketer. He made his List A debut on 10 March 2021, for Victoria in the 2020–21 Marsh One-Day Cup. Prior to his List A debut, Murphy was named in Australia’s squad for the 2020 Under-19 Cricket World Cup.

Todd Murphy is a right-arm off-spinner who plays first-class and List-A cricket for Victoria, as well as featuring for the Sydney Sixers in the Big Bash competition.

Murphy is in line for a Baggy Green next month when the Aussies head to the sub-continent for a crucial four-game series against India.

Murphy was born in Echuca and raised in the small town of Moama. Until 16, he was a top-order batsman who bowled part-time seamers, before renowned spin guru Craig Howard stepped in and realized his potential as a spinner.

Todd Murphy Career

During his rapid rise through the ranks, Murphy has represented Australia U19s and made his Shield debut for Victoria prior to his 21st birthday. 

Murphy has since featured in all three formats of domestic cricket in Australia, as well as stepping up to Australia A and Prime Minister’s XI teams earlier this summer. 

Murphy has claimed 29 wickets in just seven Sheffield Shield games at a healthy average of 25.20, putting him in the frame to be the long-term replacement for Nathan Lyon. 

The right-arm off-spinner has been included alongside Lyon, Mitchell Swepson and Ashton Agar for February’s four-game series in India, with the Aussies selecting four specialist spinners in the squad. 

Murphy beat the likes of Queensand’s Matt Kuhnemann and white-ball specialist Adam Zampa for a spot in the Aussies’ 18-man touring party.
